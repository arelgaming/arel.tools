Brand Overview

What is Arel?

Arel is a suite of web, Discord, and in-game tools for World of Warcraft raid coordination. It handles scheduling, signups, roster management, encounter planning, and attendance tracking — all connected in a single system.

The name “Arel” is a phonetic homonym for R.L. — Raid Lead. The .tools TLD reinforces the functional, no-nonsense nature of the project.

Arel is a passion project — built by one raider for the love of the game & the community. It’s free to use for up to 8 raids per month. Patreon supporters unlock unlimited raids, automation features, and early access to new tools.

Core Value Proposition

One connected system — from the calendar to the pull.

Schedule raids to collect signups. Signups feed the roster. Roster drives strats. Attendance tracks itself.

02

Voice & Tone

Principles

Arel speaks like a fellow raider who built something useful and wants to share it. The tone is clear, direct, and practical — human and personal, not polished or corporate. We write for people who already raid; we show how Arel makes running raids smoother.

We avoid exaggeration, hype, and dramatic language. We don’t glorify the Raid Lead — the focus is always on outcomes for the raid as a whole.

Toward supporters, the tone is appreciative and genuine. Their support keeps the project going, and that deserves real gratitude. The relationship is warm and community-driven.

Do / Don’t
Do
  • Be concise. Say it in one sentence if you can.
  • Use active voice. “Signups feed the roster.” Not “the roster is populated by signups.”
  • Use WoW-native language. Pull, lockout, bench, standby, strats, R.L.
  • Lead with the benefit, not the feature.
  • Trust the user. They know what a roster is.
  • Focus on outcomes for the whole raid.
  • Call Patreon backers “supporters.”
  • Acknowledge that the project is one person’s work — authenticity is an asset.
Don’t
  • Over-glorify the Raid Lead. Avoid “the role that holds it all together.”
  • Use marketing fluff: revolutionary, powerful, seamless, robust.
  • Be dramatic. “The tool your raid deserves” is too much.
  • Use exclamation marks in product copy.
  • Explain what a raid is. Assume the reader raids.
  • Use “Built by Raiders” — it’s forced.
  • Use “customer,” “subscriber,” or “user plan” language for Patreon.
  • Write like a startup. Arel is a passion project — that’s a feature, not a liability.

Color System

Philosophy

Dark, steel, and muted. No bright primaries. No decorative gradients. Contrast is achieved through value, not hue. The lightest element (#D4DAE6) is the most important and should be used sparingly — it signals the brightest, most critical point.

Rules

Cinzel is always uppercase or title case — never lowercase.

Letter-spacing on uppercase Cinzel text: minimum .08em for feature titles, up to .4em for eyebrows and micro-labels.

DM Sans body text uses weight 300. Use 400–500 for UI labels and buttons only.

No italic DM Sans. Italics in the UI use <em> styled as font-style:normal with a distinct color (--silver-lt or --silver-dk).

Don’ts

Do not recolor the N blade or center dot. Do not remove the outer ring. Do not add cardinal lines. Do not stretch from 1:1 ratio. Do not place on mid-gray without applying the mid-tone color variant.

How It Works — Four-Step Flow
ISchedule & Collect Signups — Post the raid night. Raiders sign up via Discord or web. Reminders go out automatically.
IISet the Roster — Signups populate your roster. Adjust roles, swap benches, confirm your 20.
IIIPlan Encounters — Share the encounter strat and assign individual roles. Shared with the raid before the pull — accessible on the web and in-game via addon.
IVPull. Track. Repeat. — Attendance is tracked via WCL logs. History stays on each raider. Next week starts where this one left off.

Quick Reference

Dos & Don’ts
Do
  • Use Cinzel for display and labels only
  • Keep border-radius at 0
  • Use --silver-lt sparingly for emphasis
  • Write benefit-first copy
  • Use WoW-native vocabulary
  • Scale the mark via viewBox only
  • Use 2px tight card gaps
  • Keep hover state border-based, no shadows
  • Use DM Sans 300 for body text
  • Keep the N blade at #7A8499
  • Use active voice in all copy
  • Call Patreon backers “supporters”
  • Let the passion-project nature show in the voice
Don’t
  • Use Cinzel for body or paragraph text
  • Add rounded corners anywhere
  • Use pure white (#FFF) in the dark theme
  • Lead with features over outcomes
  • Over-explain raiding to the audience
  • Stretch or distort the mark
  • Use large gutters between feature cards
  • Add drop shadows to cards or UI
  • Use exclamation marks in product copy
  • Recolor any mark elements
  • Use “Built by Raiders” or “revolutionary”
  • Use “customer,” “subscriber,” or startup-speak
